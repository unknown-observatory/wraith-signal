# Vision

Wraith Signal is a small, beautiful, browser-based exploration game about discovering a mysterious world.

The goal is not to build the largest game.

The goal is to build a focused experience that players remember.

---

# Core Vision

Players enter a strange world, explore, notice details, discover hidden things, and occasionally witness moments that feel impossible.

The world should feel like it existed before the player arrived.

The player should feel like an explorer, not a worker.

---

# Emotional Target

The primary emotion is curiosity.

Supporting emotions:

- awe;
- mystery;
- solitude;
- discovery;
- quiet social status;
- the feeling of being first.

---

# Product Direction

Wraith Signal should be:

- browser-first;
- fast to enter;
- visually memorable;
- simple enough to finish;
- polished before it is large;
- social through discovery, not forced social mechanics;
- maintainable through repository-first development.

---

# Strategic Constraint

The MVP must stay small.

The project may contain ideas for economy, lore, social systems, and long-term world evolution, but those ideas must not weaken the first playable exploration loop.

---

# Success Criteria

The MVP succeeds if players can say:

- I wanted to explore.
- I found something.
- I saw something memorable.
- I understand why another session might reveal something else.

The MVP fails if players feel they are only farming, waiting, grinding, or navigating an unfinished technical demo.
