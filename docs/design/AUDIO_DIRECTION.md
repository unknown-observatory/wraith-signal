# Audio Direction

Audio should make the world feel alive, mysterious, and worth exploring.

The MVP does not need a large audio system. It needs a clear audio foundation that supports atmosphere and discovery.

---

# Audio Pillars

## Atmosphere

The world should have presence even when the player is doing nothing.

## Restraint

Silence and space are valuable.

## Discovery Feedback

Important moments should be felt, not only seen.

## Place Identity

Audio should help areas feel distinct as the world expands.

---

# MVP Audio Scope

Current MVP audio should stay limited to existing issue scope:

- background ambience;
- player movement feedback;
- interaction sounds;
- discovery feedback;
- basic volume controls if included in settings.

---

# Discovery Audio

Discovery sounds should be clear but not arcade-like.

A cache, Wonder, or meaningful place should communicate:

- something changed;
- the moment mattered;
- the world noticed.

---

# Wonder Audio

Wonders should have distinct audio identity.

The White Stag should not need heavy music to feel magical. Subtle sound design may be stronger than obvious fanfare.

---

# Constraints

- Avoid noisy loops.
- Avoid constant music that reduces mystery.
- Avoid overproducing audio before the first gameplay loop works.
- Do not add complex adaptive music systems until required by approved scope.

---

# Open Gap

Detailed audio references, sound palette, and implementation approach are not yet defined.
