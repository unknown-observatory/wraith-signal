# World

The world is the main character of Wraith Signal.

This document records world direction without turning it into implementation scope.

---

# World Promise

The world should feel:

- ancient;
- quiet;
- beautiful;
- strange;
- partially understood;
- worth exploring.

Players should feel that something happened before they arrived.

---

# Mystery Direction

There is a hidden truth behind the world.

The game should not explain that truth immediately.

Early MVP should imply mystery through:

- places;
- atmosphere;
- discoveries;
- symbols;
- abandoned structures;
- strange natural events;
- rare Wonders.

Detailed lore delivery is not MVP scope unless explicitly approved.

---

# Starting World Direction

Historical product direction includes:

- an Observatory or HUB-like starting point;
- a forest as an early exploration area;
- a city or district as a future compact area.

Current GitHub Issues do not explicitly define Observatory, Forest, or City implementation tasks. This must be reviewed before location-specific implementation.

---

# Forest Direction

The forest is expected to be the first major emotional test of the game.

It should not be only trees and rocks.

It should support:

- exploration;
- hidden caches;
- visual mystery;
- the White Stag;
- environmental secrets;
- a first sense that the world is alive.

---

# City / District Direction

The historical direction favors a compact district over a full city.

Reason:

- lower scope;
- stronger identity;
- easier polish;
- better MVP discipline.

No full city implementation is approved by current repository planning.

---

# Wonders By Place

Wonders should match their environment.

Example principle:

- A forest Wonder should not simply reuse an ocean or sky Wonder.

The White Stag fits the forest direction.

Large future Wonders such as celestial creatures, impossible architecture, or floating phenomena belong in future scope unless approved.

---

# Lore Delivery

Lore should be discovered, not explained.

Preferred early methods:

- visual traces;
- records;
- ruins;
- environmental storytelling;
- unexplained symbols;
- historical fragments.

Avoid long exposition in the MVP.

---

# World Rules

- Keep the first world small.
- Prioritize atmosphere over size.
- Make discoveries intentional.
- Do not add biomes until the first area works.
- Preserve mystery.
