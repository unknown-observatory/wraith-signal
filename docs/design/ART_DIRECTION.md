# Art Direction

Wraith Signal should be visually memorable before it is visually large.

The MVP art direction should support exploration, mystery, and wonder.

---

# Visual Pillars

## Beauty

The world should be worth looking at.

## Mystery

The player should feel that not everything is explained.

## Simplicity

The MVP should avoid visual noise and overproduction.

## Recognition

Screenshots should be identifiable as Wraith Signal over time.

---

# MVP Visual Direction

The first playable slice should focus on:

- strong atmosphere;
- readable silhouettes;
- clean composition;
- intentional lighting;
- a small number of memorable objects or moments.

It is better to have one polished area than many unfinished areas.

---

# Wonders

Wonders are visual anchors.

They should feel rare and place-specific.

The White Stag should feel natural to the forest but still impossible enough to be remembered.

Do not reuse the same Wonder language everywhere.

---

# Screenshots

Proof-of-discovery screenshots are part of the product experience.

Visual design should consider:

- readable framing;
- clean UI overlays;
- shareable composition;
- moments that make players want to post or save an image.

---

# Constraints

- Avoid generic fantasy clutter.
- Avoid adding visual complexity before the scene performs well.
- Avoid building many biomes before one area feels strong.
- Use placeholders only when they preserve the intended composition and do not hide product risk.

---

# Open Gap

The detailed art bible is not yet defined.

Before production art begins, the project still needs:

- visual references;
- color palette;
- lighting direction;
- asset quality bar;
- first area composition rules.
